What are IISERs?

Indian Institutes of Science Education and Research (IISERs) are premier research institutes of India offering research work from the very beginning of the undergraduate degree. These institutes are comparable to the Indian Institutes of Technology in terms of education. The key difference is the focus on research â€” IISERs emphasize hands-on research at the undergraduate level.

To integrate and advance interdisciplinary science education and research, the Indian government established IISERs across India. The seven campuses of IISERs are located in Berhampur, Bhopal, Kolkata, Mohali, Pune, Thiruvananthapuram, and Tirupati. The first IISER was established in 2006 and the most recent, in 2016. These campuses continue to attract the brightest young minds in the country.

The IISERs have produced a remarkable volume of intellectual output in a short span, including numerous research papers and patents. They provide early exposure to world-class research facilities and offer interdisciplinary opportunities for extended research projects, MS theses, and internships starting at the undergraduate level.

BS and BS-MS Dual Degree Programmes

Bachelor of Science - Master of Science (BS-MS) is one of the most reputed dual degree programmes offered at IISER campuses.

The BS degree programme is offered only at IISER Bhopal. A total of 90 seats are available for BS degrees: 30 for Economic Sciences and 60 for Engineering Sciences.

Approximately 1,838 total seats are available across all seven IISER campuses for both the BS-MS dual degree and BS degree programmes.

IISER Admission Channels 2025

Admission to IISERs is offered through the following three channels:

1. IAT (IISER Aptitude Test)

  • Primary mode of admission.
  • Requires qualifying the IISER Aptitude Test 2025.
  • Open to students who have passed 10+2 with science subjects.

2. JEE Advanced Channel

  • Open to candidates in the top 15,000 All India Ranks in JEE Advanced 2025 (General category).

3. KVPY Channel (Discontinued)

Note: The KVPY channel has been discontinued. IISERs no longer admit students through this route.

Students can apply through multiple channels if eligible. Separate merit lists will be prepared for each channel.

About IAT Exam

The IISER Aptitude Test (IAT) is the entrance examination conducted by the Indian Institutes of Science Education and Research (IISERs) for admission into their BS-MS Dual Degree and BS programs. It is the primary mode of selection for candidates under the IAT 2025 Channel.

Exam Overview

  • Exam Name: IISER Aptitude Test (IAT)
  • Mode of Exam: Computer-Based Test (CBT)
  • Duration: 3 hours
  • Medium: English
  • Question Type: Multiple Choice Questions (MCQs)
  • Total Marks: 240

Eligibility

Candidates must have passed Class 12th (or equivalent) in science stream with marks above the cut-off as prescribed by IISERs for their respective boards.

IAT 2025 Registration

The application for IAT 2025 is expected to open in April/May 2025. The exam will tentatively be conducted in June 2025.

IISER Eligibility Criteria 2025

1. Educational Qualification

Candidates must have passed 10+2 (or equivalent) in the Science stream in 2023, 2024, or appearing in 2025. Required subjects include Physics, Chemistry, and Mathematics and/or Biology.

2. Minimum Marks Required

  • General / OBC-NCL / EWS: Minimum 60% aggregate marks
  • SC / ST / PwD: Minimum 55% aggregate marks

3. Admission Channel Requirements

  • IAT 2025: Must qualify the IISER Aptitude Test 2025.
  • JEE Advanced 2025: Rank within top 15,000 (General category).
  • KVPY: Discontinued â€“ not applicable from 2022 onward.

IISER Admission Process 2025 (Step-by-Step)

The admission process for IISERs in 2025 is simple and fully online. Here's how it works:

  1. Check Eligibility: Ensure you have passed Class 12 (Science stream) with required subjects and marks.
  2. Choose Your Channel: You can apply through one or more of the following:
    • IISER Aptitude Test (IAT 2025)
    • JEE Advanced 2025
  3. Register Online: Visit the official IISER admission portal and fill out the application form.
    (Expected in April/May 2025)
  4. Pay Application Fee: Pay the non-refundable application fee online (varies by category).
  5. Download Admit Card (IAT only): If applying via IAT, download your admit card once it's released.
  6. Appear for IAT 2025 (if applicable): Attend the computer-based test on the scheduled date in June 2025.
  7. Check Results: Results and ranks will be published on the IISER website.
  8. Fill Choices: Choose your preferred IISERs and programs based on your rank.
  9. Seat Allotment: Seats are allotted through multiple rounds based on your rank and preferences.
  10. Accept Offer & Join: Accept your allotted seat, pay admission fees, and join your IISER campus!

Note: Candidates must regularly check the official website for announcements and deadlines.
